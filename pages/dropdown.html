About the Transaction Close

What was announced regarding close of the transaction for the restructured ownership of Dow Corning?
  • The Dow Chemical Company announced the successful completion of the transaction to restructure ownership of Dow Corning Corporation, previously a 50:50 joint venture of Dow and Corning.
  • Dow is now 100 percent owner of Dow Corning's silicones business, which had 2015 revenues of $4.65 billion and is expected to bring $1 billion of EBITDA into Dow at full run rate synergies.
  • Dow and Corning Inc. will each maintain their current proportional indirect equity stake in Hemlock Semiconductor Group, a polysilicon producer in which Dow Corning was the majority shareholder.
  • Dow Corning is a recognized pioneer and leader in the silicones industry. Bringing together Dow and Dow Corning will accelerate growth and continue to build upon Dow Corning’s more than 70-year legacy of success.
  • Dow previously announced its intent to restructure ownership of Dow Corning's silicones business on December 11, 2015.
Why is this the right deal for Dow?
  • The transaction is a significant milestone aligned to Dow’s strategic agenda to provide clarity on our joint ventures, and it demonstrates our ongoing focus on creating shareholder value.
  • Dow Corning’s silicone position brings an entirely new chemistry to Dow and is fully aligned to Dow’s technology portfolio and strategy to go narrower and deeper into select high-growth industry segments where technology and innovation are rewarded.
  • Dow, as the new owner, will accelerate growth and bring new global reach, scale and strength to the company, while ensuring that the social and economic interests of the communities in which it operates are carefully considered and taken into account moving forward.
  • Integration strengthens the new Dow’s R&D bench, and it adds new chemistries to Dow’s portfolio and new innovative solutions for our customers.
What will happen to Hemlock Semiconductor Group?
  • Prior to this transaction, Dow Corning owned a majority interest in Hemlock Semiconductor Group and Shin-Etsu Handotai owned the remaining minority interest.
  • Upon closing, Dow and Corning Inc. will continue their long-standing partnership by each maintaining its proportional indirect equity stake in HSCG. Shin-Etsu Handotai will continue to maintain its proportional minority ownership interest in the group.
What is the value of this transaction?
  • The transaction offers significant future growth opportunities for both Dow and Dow Corning. It also brings opportunities for growth for customers, employees, suppliers, shareholders and the communities where we operate.
  • Dow Corning is recognized as the pioneer and leader in the silicone-based materials industry and this transaction will bring together that leadership with the added power of Dow’s operational excellence, global scale and reach, marketing strength and powerful innovation/R&D engine.
  • Bringing together Dow and Dow Corning will accelerate growth, which may lead to new roles and new employment opportunities over the longer term.
  • Customers can explore an even wider range of innovative and differentiated material science solutions housed under one roof.
  • As a joint venture partner since Dow Corning’s founding 73 years ago, Dow and Dow Corning have a shared history and similar values. Both headquartered in the Midland, Michigan area, Dow and Dow Corning have a heritage of supporting local charities and maintaining strong community relationships in the places where they operate.
  • As the new owner of Dow Corning, Dow will accelerate growth and bring new global reach, scale and strength to the company, while ensuring that the social and economic interests of the communities where it operates are carefully considered and taken into account moving forward.
How does this transaction fit with Dow's long-term strategy? How does this transaction benefit Dow Corning?
  • The transaction is a significant milestone aligned to Dow's commitments to accelerate growth and to optimize participation in our joint ventures.
  • Dow Corning will be an important cornerstone in building the New Dow – the world’s leading material science company.
  • Dow Corning’s silicone position brings an entirely new chemistry to Dow and is fully aligned to Dow’s technology portfolio and strategy to go narrower and deeper into select attractive industry segments where technology and innovation are rewarded.
  • Fully aligned to Dow’s portfolio strategy, Dow Corning’s silicones position deepens Dow’s product offerings in attractive end-use applications such as building and construction, consumer care, transportation, packaging, coatings, electronics and energy.
  • For Dow Corning, the added power of Dow's operational excellence, global scale, and powerful innovation and R&D engine will drive growth by enhancing Dow Corning's ability to serve customers.
What is your action plan for integration after this announcement of transaction approval?
  • There has been a joint integration team in place since we first announced our intention to restructure ownership in December 2015.
  • Both Dow and Dow Corning leaders have been working together to define the optimal business model and organization design that will ensure the current business continues to run smoothly and in an uninterrupted manner and that the growth and cost synergies are realized.
  • This team will continue with its work, and we expect the integration process to be completed within 24 months.
When is this transaction expected to be accretive?
  • The transaction is expected to be accretive to operating earnings per share (EPS), cash flow from operations and free cash flow in the first full year after the close of the transaction.
What synergies do you expect from the transaction? In what areas? Are these cost or growth synergies? How soon will they be realized?
  • Dow and Dow Corning are in a unique position to capture synergies of $400 million at annual run rates.
  • We anticipate capturing approximately $300 million in cost synergies within 24 months and approximately $100 million in growth synergies within 36 months of deal closure.
  • We anticipate Dow and Dow Corning will be a powerful combination that will accelerate growth. Growth synergies will be realized by growing in key business areas, through cross-selling opportunities and expanded R&D power.
  • While there will be job impacts resulting from the consolidation of functional roles, we also will see significant savings from the combined purchasing power of large cost areas and opportunities to share expertise across both companies.
How comfortable are you that the synergies can be realized?
  • Leaders from both companies have been highly involved in the joint planning and validation of these targets.
  • We believe that these are very realistic and achievable synergy estimates, consistent with our prior transactions and others in the sector.
  • We have a proven track record in integrating businesses and assets resulting from restructuring transactions and acquisitions and creating value from transactions, and we have consistently achieved or exceeded target synergies in transactions.
  • In addition to the cost synergies, we see significant additional upside from the revenue and other growth synergies resulting from the growth platform created by this powerful combination.
  • Growth synergies will be realized by growing in key business areas, through cross-selling opportunities and expanded R&D and innovation power.
Based on the synergies that have been announced, what are the anticipated workforce reductions?

We have not shared a job impact number, and we do not intend to do so on Day 1/June 1. The process of identifying impacted roles must take place in a careful and thoughtful manner, and the selection process will take time. With the exception of certain key leadership positions (which have already been announced), the vast majority of people decisions will be taking place in the coming weeks now that Dow Corning has joined the Dow family. It is our intention to provide specific details about the number of impacted roles we expect toward the end of the month of June, in compliance with all local laws and regulations.


Business Structure and Governance

What will happen to Dow Corning now that the transaction has closed?
  • Externally, to help make the transition as smooth as possible for customers and other key stakeholders, Dow Corning will retain its name in the near term and will transition over time to the Dow brand.
  • We see this as the best path for growth, as it enables Dow Corning to retain its well-recognized brand within the silicones industry while Dow is afforded the opportunity to branch out into a new technology platform for the Company – silicones – and strengthen its product offerings in key high-growth industry segments such as building and construction, consumer care, transportation, packaging, coatings, electronics and energy, and, over time, transition the Dow Corning brand to the Dow brand.
  • Wholly owned by Dow, Dow Corning’s silicones platform will be a key element for growth in Dow’s Infrastructure Solutions and Consumer Solutions operating segments.
Where will the Dow Corning silicones business be headquartered?

Dow Corning silicones business will maintain its current location.

Who will lead Dow Corning’s silicones business?

In addition to his current responsibilities, Howard Ungerleider, vice chairman and chief financial officer of Dow, will serve as chairman. Dow Vice President Mauro Gregorio, who now leads the Dow Corning integration, will serve as chief executive officer.

Will Dow Corning still have a market presence?

Yes, Dow Corning will continue to serve customers through its Dow Corning brand as well as the Xiameter and Molykote trademarks in the near term and will transition over time to the Dow brand. We see this as the best path for growth, as it enables Dow Corning to retain its well-recognized brand within the silicones industry while Dow is afforded the opportunity to branch out into a new technology platform for the Company – silicones – and strengthen its product offerings in key high-growth business areas such as building and construction, transportation, consumer care, coatings, electronics and energy, and, over time, transition the Dow Corning brand to the Dow brand. For customers of both Dow Corning and Dow, it will be “business as usual” on Day 1.

Will there be any restructuring or closure of Dow or Dow Corning sites?

In order to optimize asset utilization, meet our synergy targets and position the company for long-term success, it is possible we will close or consolidate some facilities. However, no decisions have been made. We are committed to keeping employees informed as soon as possible as we have additional information to share.

What will happen to the Dow Corning logo?

To help make the transition as smooth as possible for customers, Dow Corning will retain its brand and logo within the silicones industry in the near term and will transition over time to the Dow brand. The logo will remain on business cards, employee badges, signs, letterhead and stationery, customer invoices and other official business correspondence, etc. As business cards expire, a new option will be available that features the Dow-Dow Corning logo lock-up for employee use. Our goal is to make the transition to the newly restructured ownership as seamless as possible for Dow Corning customers. On Day 1, both companies will be operating business as usual.

How many Dow Corning silicones business employees are there worldwide?

Currently, there are approximately 10,000 heritage employees from the silicones business.

How many Dow employees are there worldwide?

Currently, there are approximately 49,500 heritage Dow employees.

How will integrating with Dow Corning affect Dow’s progress toward meeting its 2025 Sustainability Goals for operational excellence? How will Dow integrate Dow Corning sites into its EH&S plans?

Dow and Dow Corning share a strong commitment to safety and sustainability. Dow has taken, and will continue to take, strong steps to realize our “Vision of Zero”— no incidents, no injuries, no environmental harm. In the early days after the expected transaction close, all employees can expect to see a concentrated focus on safety. Communications discussions and training will focus on ensuring that all site employees understand EH&S expectations. We intend to ensure that all EH&S legal requirements continue to be met and that all employees are working safely with no accidents or injuries.

Dow’s 2025 Sustainability Goals affirm the Company’s commitment to protecting our planet and helping to solve some of the world’s most pressing challenges. They will be our guide as we work to improve the well-being of humanity with solutions that are good for business and good for the world. Plans for integrating Dow Corning operations into the Sustainability Goals are under review and will be communicated at a later date.

Can we have an overview of the Dow organization/structure?

Information on The Dow Chemical Company can be found by visiting Dow.com.

Is Dow more function-driven or region-driven?

Dow is a business-led organization, in which the geography implements local strategy and the functions support the business. The business portion focuses on global market influences and develops strategies for growth. The geography portion is focused on local issues; it is a conduit for information and for strategic implementation. The function portion pursues functional excellence in partnership with the business. Careers and employee development are other key focus areas for the functions.

How will Dow Corning’s silicones business be integrated into Dow?

On Day 1, Dow Corning becomes a wholly owned subsidiary of Dow. Both Dow and Dow Corning have been working to define the optimal business model and organizational design that will ensure the current business continues to run smoothly and in an uninterrupted manner, while capturing growth and cost synergy commitments in a timely and efficient fashion. The new organizational design will be comprised of two teams: the Run and Grow the Business team and the Integration & Synergies team.

Under the Run and Grow the Business team, there will be two global businesses: Dow Performance Silicones and Silicones Feedstocks and Intermediates. Dow Performance Silicones, to be led by a global business president, is chartered with ensuring ongoing operations are smooth and uninterrupted for Dow Corning customers through the integration period, while capturing growth synergies via the existing innovation pipeline, new combinatorial chemistries, expanding product offerings, leveraging complementary leadership in end-use applications, leveraging the power of the Dow geographic model, and more. Silicones Feedstocks and Intermediates, to be led by a global business director, is charged with productivity and asset management, instilling best practices to operate the grid more effectively and run to demand with an eye toward contributing to margin expansion via reducing costs in this part of the business. This business director will also serve as the primary point of contact with the Hemlock Semiconductor Group. This two-tiered approach allows us to have a dedicated and separate focus on growth while removing distractions from the day-to-day operations of Dow Corning.

Who decided the new organization of the silicones business?

The executive steering team for the Dow/Dow Corning transaction developed the new organization. Representatives from both companies were and are involved in the decision-making.

Who will be on the leadership team for the Silicones business after close?

Employee Questions – Integration Overview and Jobs

What will integration look like? How much “integration” will be done to combine the two companies?
  • Generally, Dow Corning will operate like Dow’s other businesses.
  • For example, this means, in general, that centralized functional services such as accounting, legal, tax, human resources, public affairs, information systems, etc., will be provided by the relevant Dow functions.
  • Dow Corning’s manufacturing facilities will employ Dow’s standardized operating discipline and most effective technology.
  • Because the functional organizations differ in some respects, we also anticipate that some functional job roles within Dow Corning may need to be realigned within Dow. For example, Supply Chain is a standalone function at Dow. At Dow Corning, Logistics and Supply Chain is part of Manufacturing and Engineering (MEGO).
  • Aligning the right people with the right teams is important. Not only does it maximize the productivity of the organization, it also ensures employees are exposed to career development opportunities that best capture their skills and expertise.
  • As part of our efforts to support a smooth transition, Dow and Dow Corning employees will generally continue to follow many of the human resources policies and programs of their heritage company for a period of time after Day 1. This means that, for the short term, heritage Dow employees will generally follow the current Dow human resources policies and program, and heritage Dow Corning employees will generally follow current Dow Corning human resources policies and programs. Any exceptions will be communicated.
  • In addition, there will be job impacts as we integrate. The integration team is working with leaders in both companies to determine what job roles will be realigned or consolidated to maximize the effectiveness of the organizations. The intention is to retain the best practices and people from both companies.
  • We have not shared a job impact number, and we do not intend to do so on Day 1/June 1. As we have said, the process of identifying impacted roles must take place in a careful and thoughtful manner.
  • We are committed to completing the selection process quickly, but we also owe it to all of our people to do so thoughtfully as well. These are difficult decisions.
  • It is our intention to provide specific details about the number of impacted roles we expect toward the end of the month of June, in compliance with all local laws and regulations.
  • Any individuals affected by these changes will be notified by their supervisors. We will work toward everyone in Dow Corning knowing his or her situation by the end of Q3 2016. Any Dow employees in roles that are impacted also will be made aware of their job situation by the end of Q3. Timelines may vary by country depending on local legal requirements. We are committed to communicating to employees as the process moves forward and to keeping our values of integrity and respect for people at the forefront.
Should I do anything differently now that the transaction is complete?

No. For most employees, it will be business as usual on Day 1. Unless you hear otherwise from your supervisor, your role will not change nor will the work that you do on a day-to-day basis. At this point, you should:

  • Continue to operate safely.
  • Deliver business results.
  • Reliably meet customers’ needs.
  • Continue to use the same Intranet, workstations (computers), phones, emails and printers. SAP/ERP systems and reporting tools, as well as internal computer systems applications remain separate. Use the same IT service and support providers.
  • Retain a high personal commitment to protecting Intellectual Property and locking out workstations when not in use. Company badges should be prominently displayed and guests logged.
What sort of change will this mean for Dow Corning employees?

In the short term, for most Dow Corning employees, it will be business as usual on Day 1. Employees will continue to use the same work processes, computing system, and software applications and tools. However, as the integration moves forward, some employees may experience changes such as new leaders, new organizational structures and reporting lines, new global business unit structure, new work processes, and new HR systems, among others. There is an extensive change management and training program planned to support employees throughout the transition.

Supervisors will communicate directly with employees if there is any change in job roles or significant changes in work processes. Dow is committed to keeping employees informed throughout the integration process.

What is the definition of Day 1?

Day 1 is the first day after transaction close, when Dow is the 100-percent owner of Dow Corning. Dow Corning employees become part of the Dow family.

When and how are decisions about the organization and selection for roles being made?

Functions (e.g., IT, M&E, Public Affairs, HR, R&D, etc.) and businesses are creating their organizational designs and making staffing plans – taking into account synergy targets. Leaders and HR professionals from both heritage companies are participating in this process. Both companies are working together to ensure the best fit of the organization needs and to integrate the best practices from each organization.

What are the principles driving decisions related to the organization and staffing?

This process is not a “clean slate” or “start over” approach for every organization and role. The focus is on reducing roles where there is overlap, staffing any newly created roles, and eliminating roles where necessary. The goal is to minimize disruption to the organization and to customers. Additionally, a review of a representative slate of candidates is an important consideration, as is the cost of implementation (e.g., relocations and expatriate assignments).

Will both Dow Corning and Dow employees be reviewed and considered for all positions in the Dow organization or only the open positions?

Employees from both companies will be considered for positions during the Talent Selection Process. The Talent Selection Process focuses on selecting people into the new organization needed to run the combined entity. There are overlaps, which will result in job eliminations. Including the talent from both companies is crucial for long-term success.

When will employees know their employment status?

We will work toward everyone in Dow Corning knowing his or her situation by the end of Q3 2016. Any Dow employees impacted will also be notified by end of Q3 2016. Timelines may vary by country depending on local legal requirements.

Who will communicate to me if I have a job or not?

Your supervising manager or his/her designee will be involved with all notifications in coordination with HR.

For the vast majority of employees, the notification process will begin around the end of June. We will work toward everyone in Dow Corning knowing his or her situation by the end of Q3 2016. Timelines may vary by country depending on local legal requirements. We are committed to communicating to employees as the process moves forward and to keeping our values of integrity and respect for people at the forefront.

What will my organization look like? Who is my boss?

Our intention is to let you know as soon as possible. But unless you hear otherwise, it’s “business as usual.” We will communicate all changes in advance of their implementation. For now, we should concentrate on working safely and meeting our customers’ needs.

  • Relevant work experiences
  • Critical skills and competencies required in the new organization.

In some, but not all cases, interviews will be necessary to make decisions.

The growth of the company will lead to more opportunities and options for employees in the long term.

Employees of Dow Corning’s silicones business will have the same opportunity to carve out a unique and vibrant career as other Dow employees.

We also believe that being part of a company with increased scale and geographic scope will offer more opportunities for employees over the long term. The new Dow will be able to offer employees more opportunities in an organization with increased scale and geographic scope. Growth of the company means more opportunities and options for employees in the long term.

Will Dow Corning employees be able to use Dow’s internal posting system (JAS) to apply for jobs after Day 1?

However, a team is in place working to enable visibility to all open roles at Dow as soon as practical. In the meantime, HR professionals will facilitate review of relevant candidates for open roles across both companies.

What are “cost reduction synergies”?

Both companies have resources dedicated to doing the same kind of work. These areas of overlap can be combined, resulting in savings, known in the financial world as cost reduction synergies. While cost synergies will result in some near-term job impacts, savings will also come from functional synergy captures such as combined purchasing power for large cost areas such as utilities, supply chain, freight, and logistics costs, raw material savings, and opportunities for expertise sharing across both companies.

There also will be savings with organizational streamlining and the consolidation of central functions. This will likely result in some job impacts. Dow remains committed to its values of integrity and respect for people and to keeping employees informed during this process. All of these things will contribute to a minimum of $300 million in synergies, which we announced December 11, 2015. Dow and Dow Corning also expect to capture another $100 million in growth synergies.

Can you share your plans for growth synergies?

We anticipate Dow and Dow Corning will be a powerful combination that will accelerate growth. Growth synergies will be realized by growing in key business areas, through cross-selling opportunities and expanded R&D and innovation power.

An employee who declines a role may be eligible for severance under limited circumstances, including distance of the relocation required and job level reduction, if any. These decisions will be made in accordance with Dow and Dow Corning HR policies or programs and in compliance with each country’s applicable local legislation.

If my role is being eliminated, can I be considered for another role? Are open jobs listed somewhere?

This is not a voluntary reduction in the workforce and the primary decisions for reductions will be related to synergies, subject to local laws. Please direct any questions to your leader or Human Resources for more discussion.

Notification requirements vary according to local law and transition needs. If you are impacted, timing will be part of the notification process.

We are committed to keeping you informed throughout the process. Job changes are not a guarantee, and are always dependent on performance and qualifications for a position. Development is a key enabler for both the company’s and your success.

How will career development be affected?

Career development continues to be a key process required for both company and individual success. How does this action make room for future growth for Dow, in this region?

Bringing together Dow and Dow Corning is an opportunity to combine the complementary strengths of two leading companies. As the new owner, Dow will accelerate growth and bring new global reach, scale and strength to Dow Corning, while ensuring that the social and economic interests of the communities where they operate are carefully considered and taken into account moving forward.

Impacts EMEAI (Europe, Middle East, Africa and India)

How will Dow Corning’s restructure impact business in the EMEAI region?
  • Dow in Europe has a strong and established customer base. Over half of our global strategic customers, often important innovators in their own right and iconic brand owners, are headquartered in Europe.
  • The addition of Dow Corning’s silicone position creates an even broader and more sophisticated solution offering, which together we will use to serve our customers in the EMEAI region, both new and existing, in high-growth and attractive end-use applications such as building and construction, consumer care, transportation, packaging, coatings, electronics and energy, among others.
  • With the addition of Dow Corning, Dow’s footprint across the EMEAI region will be enhanced significantly, increasing our manufacturing presence in Belgium, France, Germany and the UK and adding production capability in India, bringing us even closer to our customers.

Retirees

What will be the impact of this transaction on Dow Corning’s sites?

On Day 1, it will be business as usual at our sites, and our priorities on safety remain the same. As the integration moves forward, we will work together to define the organization that will support Dow’s and Dow Corning’s growth. Both companies are working together to retain the best practices and best people from each organization, and we are committed to sharing more information when it is available.

Will the Dow Corning brand name be going away?

No. Externally, the Dow Corning brand will be maintained in the near term to help ease the transition for customers and other key stakeholders; over time, it will transition to the Dow brand. Dow Corning will continue to serve customers through the Dow Corning brand and through Xiameter and Molykote trademarks in the near term. We see this as the best path for growth, as it enables Dow Corning to retain its well-recognized brand within the silicones industry while Dow is afforded the opportunity to branch out into a new technology platform for the Company – silicones – and strengthen its product offerings in key high-growth end-use applications such as building and construction, transportation, consumer care, packaging, coatings, electronics and energy.

How does this transaction impact my Dow Corning pension?

Former Dow and Dow Corning employees will remain in their current pension plan after close. Future changes, if any, will be communicated prior to their effective date.

How will I be notified of changes that Dow makes to retiree benefits?

Any changes will be communicated through direct mail to your address. Changes also will be posted on DowFriends.com. If you are a deferred vested participant in the Dow Corning’s pension plan, it’s critical that you continue to provide Dow with up-to-date contact information.
